How do you handle and supply packaging?

Do you supply goods to the UK market in primary, secondary, or shipment packaging under your own brand?

What does this mean?

If your organisation makes goods available for purchase in UK shops or online stores under the company’s brand name, you should answer yes.

Primary packaging is what goods are contained in when supplied to customers. This is known as a ‘sales unit’.

Secondary packaging is used to group several primary packaged ‘sales units’. Organisations may use it to display goods in shops. It can also be used to ship single or multiple items directly to customers.

Shipment packaging is a type of secondary packaging that is used to ship single or multiple sales units directly to consumers. This includes cardboard boxes and mailing bags.

Do you pay another organisation to produce, pack or place goods on the UK market that are branded under your name?

What does this mean?

You should answer yes if you sell goods that are branded under your name, but you have paid another company to carry out part of the supply chain for you.

For example, you may have paid another organisation to do any of the following:

  1. produce goods that will be sold under your brand name
  2. pack goods that will be sold under your brand name
  3. place your branded goods on the UK market

Do you use transit packaging to transport goods that are placed on the UK market?

Transit packaging is also known as tertiary packaging.

Do you place goods into packaging, on behalf of a UK organisation that is not obligated under extended producer responsibility for packaging?

What does this mean?

Some organisations fall under the threshold for the regulations, which means they are not legally obligated. This includes organisations that:

  1. have an annual turnover of less than £2 million a year
  2. handle or supply less than 50 tonnes of packaging a year

If an organisation pays you to place their goods into packaging, and that organisation falls under the threshold, you should answer ‘yes’.

Do you import packaging that contains goods into the UK to sell?

What does this mean?

You should answer yes if your organisation imports products from outside the UK that are in primary and secondary packaging and goes on to supply these products to the UK market.

You should answer yes if your organisation:

  1. imports products from outside the UK that are in primary and secondary packaging
  2. goes on to supply these products to the UK market

Secondary packaging includes shipment packaging. Shipment packaging is used to send single or multiple sales units to consumers. This includes things like mail bags and cardboard boxes.

You should answer yes even if you discard packaging before selling the goods.

Do you own an online marketplace that allows non-UK businesses to sell packaged products in the UK?

What’s an online marketplace?

An online marketplace is a website that has been set up to allow non-uk organisations to sell their goods online (for example, Amazon or Etsy).

If your organisation owns a website that sells goods from UK organisations only, this is not classed as an online marketplace.

Do you sell empty packaging to businesses that are not obligated under extended producer responsibility for packaging?

How to tell if an organisation isn’t obligated

Some organisations fall under the threshold for the regulations, which means they are not legally obligated. This includes organisations that:

  • have an annual turnover of less than £2 million a year
  • handle or supply less than 50 tonnes of packaging a year

Do you hire out or lend reusable packaging to UK organisations?

For example, some organisations hire out or lend wooden pallets to other organisations for transporting goods. The wooden pallets are returned after use and loaned out again.
