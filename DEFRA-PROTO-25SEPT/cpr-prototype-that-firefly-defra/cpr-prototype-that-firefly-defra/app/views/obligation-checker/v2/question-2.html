How do you handle or supply packaging?

By answering these questions about how you work with packaging, you’ll find out if your organisation is affected by the new regulations, and how.

Do you supply your own-brand goods to the UK market in primary or secondary packaging?

What does this mean?

Your organisation supplies own-brand goods to the UK market if you are responsible for making goods available for purchase in UK shops or online stores under your brand.

Primary packaging is what goods are contained in when supplied to customers. This is known as a ‘sales unit’.

Secondary packaging is used to group several primary packaged ‘sales units’. Organisations may use it to display goods in shops. It can also be used to ship single or multiple items directly to customers.

Do you pay another organisation to pack and place your own-brand goods on the UK market?

You should answer yes if another organisation produces and packs goods for you, which you go on to sell under your own brand name.

Do you use transit packaging to transport goods that are placed on the UK market?

Do you import packaging that contains goods into the UK to sell?

You should answer yes if your organisation imports goods from outside the UK that are stored within primary and secondary packaging, and goes on to supply these goods to the UK market.

Do you own an online marketplace that allows non-UK businesses to sell packaged products in the UK?

What’s an online marketplace?

An online marketplace is a website that has been set up to allow other companies to sell their goods online. In this instance, you are facilitating the sale of third-party brands and not your own-brand goods.

Owning an online marketplace is not the same as selling packaged goods on your organisation’s own-branded online store.

Do you sell empty packaging to organisations that are not affected by the extended producer responsibility regulations?

How to tell if an organisation is affected by the regulations

Some organisations are not affected by the regulations. This includes organisations that:

  • have an annual turnover of less than £1 million a year
  • handle or supply less than 50 tonnes of packaging a year
