Guidance Report takeaway cups data: information for take-back schemes

As a take-back scheme, you will need to report data for organisations who sell drinks in takeaway cups and support them with their responsibilities.

This is part of the extended producer responsibility (EPR) for packaging regulations, which encourages organisations to recycle.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Enviroment Agency

Published: Published 15 June 2022

Organisations who sell drinks in takeaway cups can ask you to report data on the cups that they recycle.

To support these organisations with their cup recycling, you’ll need to:

  • get approved and register as a take-back scheme
  • pay yearly registration fees
  • setup an account to use the online service to report data

What you need to do as a take-back scheme

If you work with organisations who must report takeaway cups data, you will need to:

  • support your customers to make arrangements for their used cups to be collected and recycled
  • help your customers meet their obligations and ensure they are compliant with EPR
  • report takeaway cups data for your customers

You’ll use the new online service to submit this data.

Reporting data on takeaway cups

You will need to use the online service to report 2 pieces of data twicer per year:

  • the weight of cups that your customer has sold
  • the weight of cups that your customer has sent for recycling

How much it costs to be a take-back scheme

As a take-back scheme you must pay a registration fee of £3,726 every year to each individual nation your customers operate in.

You must also pay £74 for each customer you work with. This fee must be paid individually to each nation your customer operates in.

Organisations you might work with

Your customers will be organisations for example:

  • cafes
  • coffee shops
  • airlines
  • leisure centres
  • universities
  • transport hubs like stations and airports

Types of cups that apply

You need to report data about your customers’ fibre-based composite cups. These are cups made of paper fibre with a polyethylene (PE) lining. These cups are used to sell takeaway drinks, such as coffee.

You do not need to report data for your customers on other takeaway cups, such as:

  • Bamboo cups
  • cups that are only plastic
  • cups that are only paper

You’ll use the new online service to submit this data.

How you can support your customers with takeaway cups

You can provide additional support to help your customers:

  • arrange collection of the takeaway cups in their recycling bins.
  • get the weight of cups they have gathered for recycling.

Approval process to become a take-back scheme

You need to follow the approval process to become a take-back scheme

1. Identify which nation you’ll register with

You will need to register to become a take-back scheme to help organisations report data on takeaway cups.

You must apply to the UK nation where your registered office is based. For example, if your registered office is based in London, you only need to apply to be a take-back scheme in England.

2. Contact the environmental regulator for your nation

In England

  • Environmental Agency (EA)
  • enquiries@environment-agency.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03708 506 506

In Scotland

  • Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA)
  • enquiries@environment-agency.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03708 506 506

In Wales

  • Natural Resources Wales
  • enquiries@naturalresourceswales.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03000 996 699

In Northern Ireland

  • Department of Agriculture, Environment and Rural Affairs (DAERA)
  • daera.helpline@daera-ni.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 0300 200 7850

3. Getting your application reviewed

The environmental regulator will review your application and tell you if you are approved.

If you are approved the environmental regulator will email you with an approval code.

If you lose this approval code you will need to contact the same environmental regulator to request a new one.

Creating an account and signing in to report data

You will need to set up an account in the online service with your approval code.
