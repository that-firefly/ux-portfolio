Guidance Report takeaway cups recycling: information for drink sellers

If you sell drinks in takeaway cups, you must report on the weight of cups you have sold, and the weight of cups collected for recycling. This is part of extended producer responsibility (EPR) regulations.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Enviroment Agency

Published: Published 15 June 2022

Extended producer responsibility (EPR) for packaging regulations encourages organisations to recycle. This includes takeaway cups. 

You’ll need to report data on certain kinds of takeaway cups as part of EPR if:

  • you're an organisation who fills drinks in takeaway cups and sells them
  • you have premises in the UK, which includes anywhere you sell those drinks
  • you employ 10 or more full time or equivalent staff during a whole calendar year
Charities and individual franchisees do not need to register or report data. If you’re a franchisee your parent franchise company will report takeaway cups data on your behalf.

You’ll use a new online service to report and submit this data twice every year.

What takeaway cups apply

You need to report data on the fibre-based composite cups. These are cups made of paper fibre with a polyethylene (PE) lining. For example, these cups are used to sell takeaway drinks such as coffee.

You do not need to report data on other takeaway cups, such as:

  • bamboo cups
  • cups that are only made of plastic
  • cups that are only made of paper

What you need to do if you sell drinks in takeaway cups

By 30 September 2024, you must be registered so that you can:

  • install a recycling bin to collect the cups 
  • record the weight of cups in kilograms that are sold and cups that are collected 
  • make arrangements for the used cups to be collected and recycled 
  • submit the data to the appropriate environmental regulator for every nation your organisation operates in
You must pay £141 per year to meet your requirements to collect and report takeaway cups. You must make this payment to the environmental regulator in the nation where your organisation is registered.

Installing a recycling bin

You’ll need a dedicated recycling bin for takeaway cups. It should only contain empty takeaway cups.

There should be no additional waste, such as:

  • lids, cups sleeves and stirrers
  • liquids
  • other waste packaging

 Your recycling bin must be visible to the public and easily accessible.

Using a take-back scheme

Businesses called take-back schemes can:

  • collect the cups
  • arrange the recycling
  • report the data you record
  • submit the data to the regulator

What data you need to collect and report

You'll have to collect and report the weight of cups that you’ve: 

  • sold
  • sent for recycling 
If you sell cups in more than one nation in the UK, you’ll have to give the weight sold in each nation.

You must report twice a year using the online service.

Keep your records in kilograms. You must keep data records for 7 years.

When you can report your cups data

These dates are for collecting and submitting data in 2024 and 2025:

  • First data collection period is from 1 October 2024 to 30 June 2025
  • First data submission period is from 1 July 2024 to 1 October 2025
