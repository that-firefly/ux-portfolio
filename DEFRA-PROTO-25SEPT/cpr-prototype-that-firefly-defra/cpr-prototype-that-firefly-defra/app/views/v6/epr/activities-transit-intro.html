Did your organisation place any transit packaging on the UK market in 2022?

{{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "View definition and examples of transit packaging", html: '

Definition

Transit packaging is what sales units or goods grouped in secondary packaging are contained in while they’re being transported or handled. It’s used to prevent damage to the goods.

Transit packaging does not include road, rail, ship and air containers.

Example A

A chemical manufacturer sells barrels of the chemical they produce to a pharmaceutical organisation. The manufacturer transports the barrels to the pharmaceutical organisation on shrink-wrapped crates.

The transit packaging is the shrink-wrap and crates.

Example B

An online marketplace facilitates the sale of clothing by a non-UK based producer. The online marketplace adds bubble wrap and a cardboard box to the clothing, to deliver it to the end consumer.

The transit packaging is the bubble wrap and cardboard box.
