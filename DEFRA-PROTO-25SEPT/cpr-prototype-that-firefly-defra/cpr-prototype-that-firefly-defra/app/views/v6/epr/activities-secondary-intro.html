{{ govukDetails({ summaryText: "View definition and examples of secondary packaging", html: '

Definition

Secondary packaging is used to group a number of ‘sales units’. Organisations sometimes use it to display goods in shops or sell it to the end consumer along with the goods it contains.

Example A

An organisation sells individually boxed ink cartridges, grouped in a cardboard tray covered in plastic wrap, to a retail organisation.

The retail organisation removes the plastic wrap, disposes of it and displays the ink cartridges on shelves in the cardboard trays. It sells the ink cartridges individually.

The secondary packaging is the cardboard trays and the plastic wrap.

Example B - not secondary packaging

An organisation sells individually boxed ink cartridges, grouped in a cardboard tray covered in plastic wrap, to an end consumer.

In this example, the cardboard tray and plastic wrap are primary packaging, because they’re sold to the end consumer as part of the sales unit.
